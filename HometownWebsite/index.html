Beyond its historical sites, luxury resorts, and vibrant nightlife.

The second-largest metropolitan area in the Philippines, Cebu is known as a fishing village before the Spanish invasion. The name “Cebu” has been interpreted in many ways, its rich history indicates that it is derived from the term “Sibu,” which means “Trade.” Because of its coastal location, was referred to as "Sinibuayng Hingpit," which translates to "the place for trading." Later, the locals became accustomed to abbreviating and using Sibu or Sibo (to deal). As a result of its gradual industrialization throughout time, Cebu province is currently among the most developed in the Philippines.

Cebu is a “power island” in every sense of the word.
